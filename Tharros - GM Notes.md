## Major Nations

### **1\. Virelia *(Centralized, bureaucratic, coastal empire)***

* **Primary Culture:** *Serenei* (see above)

* **Racial Mix:** Predominantly Human, Galapa, and Inferis

* **Identity:** Civilized, cosmopolitan, proud of their cities, art, and religion

* **Religion:** The Eternal Flame is state-sanctioned

* **Traits:** Educated elites, slick diplomats, clean uniforms, high architecture

* **Minorities:** Pockets of Drakona immigrants, older Dwarven enclaves

* **Tharros Connection:** The Church, Brotherhood, and Syndicate all have Virelian roots

---

### **2\. Korralt *(Frontier kingdom, rich in woodlands and hills)***

* **Primary Culture:** *Panthyr*

* **Racial Mix:** Dwarves, Humans, some Halflings

* **Identity:** Stubborn, honest, community-first, strong oral traditions

* **Religion:** Loosely follows the Flame, with strong regional saints

* **Traits:** Long stories, thick embroidery, warm drinks, loyal to kin

* **Minorities:** Nomadic Galapa clans, especially along rivers

* **Tharros Connection:** Many ordinary settlers came from here, especially to escape taxes and famine

---

### **3\. Skaldmere *(Cold, fragmented coastal lands)***

* **Primary Culture:** *Nordahr*

* **Racial Mix:** Humans, Drakona, some Half-Orcs

* **Identity:** Proud seafarers, austere, survival-focused, deeply tribal

* **Religion:** Shamanic traditions mix with a distant respect for the Flame

* **Traits:** Tattoos, ritual duels, sea ballads

* **Tharros Connection:** Mercenaries and Brotherhood bodyguards often hail from here

* **Primary Culture:** *Tharain*

* **Racial Mix:** Humans, Inferis, and Elves

* **Identity:** Scholarly, poetic, fatalistic; deeply interested in history

* **Religion:** Flame is present, but often seen metaphorically

* **Traits:** Riddles, layered robes, relic obsession, ruined libraries

## View of Tharros

*“It’s out there, beyond the common trade lanes. Real enough if you know where to look. Just not the kind of place that makes it into textbooks or tour routes.”*

#### **Mysterious but Real**

Tharros is not a secret, but neither is it a household name. It's *the place everyone’s heard of, but few can point to on a map*. Some treat it as a curiosity; others believe it's more myth than fact. There are ports that quietly send ships west—but no one makes it a routine.

* *“My cousin went to Tharros. Or so he said. His letters stopped after the third moon.”*

* *“You can book passage there, sure. You just need to know the right harbormaster, and pay like you’re buying a secret.”*

#### **The Syndicate’s Quiet Interest**

The **Ironbound Syndicate** operates outposts and controls trade routes. But they’re *deliberate in keeping Tharros off the radar*. They avoid attracting state or rival corporate attention, presenting the island as unremarkable.

* *“Expansion territory, minimal output. Not worth inspection.”*

* Internal memos, though, speak of “exceptional potential,” “anomalous energies,” and “competitive embargoes.”

#### **The Church’s Soft Warnings**

The **Church of the Eternal Flame** acknowledges Tharros, but publicly speaks of it as spiritually barren or unstable. Missionaries go, but not the promising ones. Sermons frame it as a place of moral *testing*, or temptation.

* *“The Flame burns weak where the soil remembers other fires.”*

* Some heretical sects believe Tharros is the *cradle* of true divinity. The Church condemns these ideas.

#### **The Scholar’s Obsession**

Academics refer to Tharros in footnotes and fringe journals. The **Remnant Brotherhood** has seeded misinformation while pursuing relics and ruins in secret. Tharros is whispered about in university halls, often with mocking smiles.

* *“Tharros? The island with singing rocks and blinking ruins? Yes, I did a paper on it once.”*

* *“No one ever publishes serious work on Tharros. Those who try… usually stop writing.”*

#### **The Common Folk's View**

For most people, Tharros is a foggy idea. Dangerous? Maybe. Exotic? Probably. Worth your life? Unlikely.

* *“It’s out west, yeah? Past the old chain. Some folks strike it rich. Others don’t come back.”*

* *“Too many storms. Too many secrets. The sea doesn’t like giving it back.”*

# Monster Ideas

## **Touched / Changed / Forgotten**
<!-- CATEGORY: CREATURE -->

These are people—or things that used to be people—transformed by their proximity to powerful Relics or exposure to Reckonings. They're tragic, unsettling, and often still *almost human*.

### **Examples:**

* **Echo-Walkers**: Former explorers who became attuned to a Relic's ambient field. Their minds drift in and out of time. They speak in half-sentences from the past and future. They don’t seem to realize they’re attacking.

* **Hollowed**: Humans with no eyes or mouths, only smooth skin. Their thoughts were taken, not their lives. They move with purpose, but no will.

* **The Repeating Man**: A single figure seen in many parts of the island—always identical, always silent. Is it one being or many instances?

These aren't always combat encounters. They're mysteries, omens, or puzzles to interpret.

## **Relic-Born / Anomalous Beasts**

Creatures warped or created by the Relics' ambient effects. Their form reflects natural species, but twisted by the strange forces they nest near.

### **Examples:**

* **Stitch-Crows**: Birds that steal bones and twine them into nests—and then walk in them.

* **False-Harts**: Deer-like beasts whose antlers are tangled with wires and stone. Their bodies shift subtly in reflection, as if they were made from memory, not matter.

* **Mimics of the Deep**: Predators that don’t attack unless you say a certain word, or forget a memory. They're not traps—they’re triggers.

### **Lacuna Serpents**

*"They don’t crawl. They skip."*

Worm-like creatures that *blink* short distances instead of slithering. Where they pass, time stutters—sound repeats, torches flicker backward, wounds reopen. Killing them causes a brief, localized time-loop.  
 Some say they’re not native to the island, but *left behind* by something else.

* **Twist**: If tracked for too long, you might loop back to where you started without realizing it.

  ---

### **Pulse Elk**

*"It’s not the steps you hear. It’s the echoes of the heart."*

Tall, six-legged ungulates with translucent skin, showing a faintly glowing inner structure. Their antlers vibrate with constant low-frequency hums. They attract other animals—but terrify humans.  
 Standing near one may sync your heartbeat to theirs, leading to arrhythmias, hallucinations, or sudden emotional floods.

* **Twist**: Killing one sends a psychic shockwave across a wide area. Some say other creatures come to mourn.

  ---

### **Threadborn**

*"They walk like puppets, but dance like gods."*

Creatures assembled from bones, moss, bits of fur, and rope-like strands of unknown origin. Seem to be *built* by the forest. Not aggressive—until you disturb a Relic nearby. Then they move with uncanny grace and preternatural coordination.

* **Twist**: If dismembered, the parts continue to move independently—and try to reform.

  ---

### **Mirrorhowl**

*"They wear your face. Not like a mask. Like a choice."*

Predatory creatures that reflect back the image of someone nearby—but *slightly wrong*. They approach camps, mimicking loved ones or party members. Their goal isn’t to kill—it’s to replace.  
 Some don’t realize they've been replaced until much later. Maybe never.

* **Twist**: Their true form can only be seen in surfaces that distort (rippling water, cracked mirrors, warped glass).

  ---

### **Ashplume Moths**

*"They come with the fog. They leave with the names."*

Swarming insects made of chitin and ash. Individually harmless, but they move in clouds near dormant Relics. People exposed for too long forget pieces of language—names, verbs, even how to write.  
 In one village, a chronic infestation led to an entire generation unable to speak proper grammar.

* **Twist**: Killing one restores a word—but not necessarily the *right* one.

  ---

### **Gestalt Husk**

*"It remembers more than it should."*

A gray-skinned humanoid that shouldn't exist. It speaks in multiple voices. It knows things it shouldn’t. Its movements are clumsy—like it's still learning its body.  
 No one agrees where it came from, or what it wants. Some say it’s a prototype. Others, a failed simulation of a person. It may try to *join* with others to "complete itself".

* **Twist**: It gains fragments of personality from those it touches. Some feel sympathy. Others feel... recognition.  
  

These beasts might protect a Relic site or roam dead zones. You might not understand what they are until it’s far too late.

## **The Unknowable (Rare / Mythic Tier)**

Used *sparingly*, these are the “deep ones,” the beings that should not be approached, understood, or perhaps even seen. They are the island's immune system—or its memories.

### **Examples:**

* **The Lantern Below**: A subterranean glow mistaken for a Relic, which lures people in. Those who see it dream of a city that cannot exist.

* **The Monolith Shepherd**: A being seen only when too many have gathered near a particular ruined spire. Those who witness it stop speaking of it. Some stop speaking entirely.

* **A Name With No Mouth**: A sound that leaves a physical scar. Known only to those who fail to forget it.

These beings bend perception, break logic, and rewrite purpose.

## Reckonings: When the Island Responds
<!-- CATEGORY: MECHANIC, LORE -->

**Reckonings** are the Island’s most feared—and least understood—phenomena. They are not weather events, nor curses, nor divine punishments. They are… responses. Reflexes. System failures. Rituals forgotten but still running.

They happen irregularly. Sometimes months apart, sometimes a quiet ripple between worse storms. Often small—just a missing person, a village that goes silent. But sometimes… far greater.

Some believe they are the **Island’s immune system**, reacting to disruption. Others think they are **the last echoes of the Ancients' systems**, set to purge or cleanse. A few claim they are **tests**, and even fewer think they’re **messages**.

They are not predictable.  
But they are not *random*.

---

### How Frequent?

* **Minor Reckonings**: Every few weeks somewhere on the island—sicknesses, anomalies, brief creature migrations, lights in the sky.

* **Major Reckonings**: Once or twice a year. These might leave craters, erase towns, or alter entire regions. People go missing. Maps become wrong.

* Some suspect a **Grand Reckoning** looms—though few dare name what that means.

## Origin of Races – Designed, Evolved, or Dreamed?

<!-- CATEGORY: LORE -->
*From “The Shape of Us” – A Philosophical Dialogue Among Scholars*

There are elves, dwarves, goblins, orcs, humans—and others besides. We speak, build, war, and wonder. But why do we differ?

One school of thought holds that the races are divine creations, each shaped by a different god with distinct purpose and temperament. Another claims they are biological variations, shaped by environment, time, and adaptation.

Some whisper that the Ancestors made us—that the Relics were not tools, but wombs. That we were grown, not born. And still others suggest an even stranger truth: that the world itself dreams us, and that our differences are echoes of forgotten stories.

The Church teaches that all people are children of the Flame, though some bear heavier burdens than others. The Assembly insists that all life is sacred, shaped by the land, and must be allowed to return to it.

Whatever the truth, our forms are many. But our questions are the same.

## Divinity – Flame, Fragments, or Fabrication?

*From “Many Faces, One Fire?” – Disputed Scroll of the Third Illumination*

There are gods—this much most people believe. But whether they are many or one, mortal or eternal, remains ever debated.

The Church of the Eternal Flame teaches that all divine power is but one light, refracted through different names and symbols. According to them, the Eternal Flame is both origin and destiny—the ordered fire from which creation rose and to which it must return.

Other faiths persist, especially in the margins: river gods, stone spirits, ancestral whispers. Some hold that the Relics themselves are divine—or at least touched by divinity.

Skeptics point to the inconsistencies. If gods are real, why are they silent? Why do miracles vary? Some suggest the gods are dead. Others that they never were, and belief alone sustains the rites and visions.

Still, most cling to some version of faith. Not always because it explains, but because it comforts. Whether the Flame is truth or symbol, it continues to burn.

## **🌿 Veiled Assembly – Reframed**

**Moral Core**: The island is alive. You’re trespassing.  
 **Public Face**: Protectors of balance and ancient wisdom.  
 **Reality**: Eco-radicals who will destroy what you build if it threatens the land.

---

### **🌱 Political Posture**

* **Openly opposed** to the Brotherhood and Syndicate—calls them both “colonial parasites,” albeit using more poetic language when needed.

* Tries to win the hearts of common settlers in **Marrowhold** and nearby homesteads by offering **free healing, guidance, and safe passage**—often before the factions even respond.

* Believes the people of Tharros (especially new settlers) can be **taught to live in balance**, but if they resist, they become **acceptable sacrifices**.

---

### **💣 Radical Core**

* Operates **direct sabotage missions**: blowing up bridges, poisoning machines, and freeing captive relics.

* Refers to these actions as “cleansings,” “ritual corrections,” or “symbiotic disruption.”

* Hides weapons and Relic-based tools in the roots of ancient trees. Their hit cells are **small, autonomous**, and always vanish afterward.

---

### **🧠 Propaganda Strategy**

* Distributes **parables and cautionary poems** through wandering bards and herbalists.

* Claims their extreme actions are **the island’s will**, not their own.

* Regularly spread rumors that **Brotherhood experiments have caused Reckonings**, or that **Syndicate drill sites are leaking madness**.

---

### **🔥 Internal Conflict**

* Not all Assembly members agree with Maera’s militant path.

* Some are trying to **build a cooperative model**, especially with sympathetic settlers and druids who aren’t ready to take up arms.

* Others think she’s **not going far enough**, and want to begin “pruning” Marrowhold itself before it becomes too dangerous.

---

## **🌿 Thistleshade – Reframed Settlement**

**Population**: \~100 core (radicals, scouts, stewards)  
 **Up to 200+ seasonal allies, sympathizers, refugees**

### **🧬 Origins**

* A convergence point of people who **felt the island call to them**, mostly defectors, outcasts, and a few scholars who turned against their orders.

* Located in the **dense interior**, half built into cliffs, canopies, and hollowed-out stone arches overgrown with glowing moss.

### **🌼 Surface Image**

* Beautiful, serene commune. Living gardens. Flowing springs.

* Open-air teaching circles on herbalism, history, and “how to listen to the land.”

* No one raises a voice. Everyone listens. **It feels like being accepted by something ancient.**

### **🌑 Beneath the Surface**

* Some rooms are **sealed off with living roots**.

* “Foundlings” who appear after Reckonings are **tested** in rituals no outsider is told about.

* All guests are watched, even those who think they’re friends.

* Anyone asking “too much” is offered tea… and a walk.

### **🌿 Thistleshade *(Assembly-Aligned Wildhold)***

**Population**: \~100 permanent, 150–200 shifting seasonal population  
 **Tone**: Harmonious, spiritual, self-sufficient—but suspicious of change  
 **Public Motto**: *“Walk gently. Take only what you can carry in silence.”*

#### **🧬 Origins:**

* Founded by early settlers who claimed to feel the island's "breath" and “mood.” Former healers, fugitives, and dreamers.

* Embraced by the Veiled Assembly as **proof that coexistence was still possible**.

#### **😊 Surface Image:**

* A calm, serene commune—**all are welcome** (if they behave).

* Shared meals, open-air gathering circles, botanical gardens of natural remedies.

* Teachers, herbalists, and spiritual guides offer aid to anyone who approaches peacefully.

#### **🧨 Below the Surface:**

* Outsiders are gently “redirected” if they stay too long or ask the wrong questions.

* The Assembly has **sabotaged other settlements** and has an internal “circle of the root” that vets everything.

* Some plants used in healing are **not legal**—and some are not explainable.

#### **💡 Player Perception:**

* First impression: **safe haven in a hostile land**.

* Long-term: **strange coincidences, disappearances, and "natural accidents."**

## **🌊 The Settlement History of Tharros**
<!-- CATEGORY: LORE, SECRET -->

Tharros is **not home**—it's a **threshold**. Across centuries, it has **drawn people in**, yet **never let them remain**.

---

### **🏺 1\. The Ancient Precursor Civilization (Unknown Era)**

The first and only true “native” society was the **Precursor Civilization**, long gone before memory or record. They built or used the **Relics**. Their cities are now **half-buried**, deformed, or fused with the land.

* Their downfall is thought to be the result of **overuse or mismanagement** of the Relics.

* Some say they triggered a **grand Reckoning** that made Tharros what it is now: unstable, haunted, unhealing.

* Their ruins are still buried in the jungle, mountains, or beneath the ground—whispers of a world that **shouldn’t have existed**.

*Nothing living survived.* What remains may not be alive.

---

### **🛶 2\. Waves of Later Settlement**

Over the centuries, various groups tried to claim Tharros. None succeeded.

#### **a) The Pale Colonies (\~300 years ago)**

An organized imperial outpost: fortified town, harbor, watchtowers.

* Goal: harvest timber, extract minerals, establish dominion.

* Vanished within a generation. **Ruins remain**, reclaimed by the jungle.

* The last recorded message: “We see fire in the rain. It comes again.”

#### **b) The Flame Disciples (\~150 years ago)**

A religious sect drawn by prophecies. They believed Tharros was a place of divine trial.

* Built monastic camps near hot springs and mountains.

* May have activated dormant Relics in pursuit of “ascension.”

* Their relics and bones are occasionally found in **perfect circles of fused stone**.

#### **c) The Silent Trade Enclave (\~80 years ago)**

Independent traders and pirates tried to use the island as a secret port and smuggling hub.

* Coastal warehouses, hidden coves, black markets.

* Disappeared within a decade. Their maps are **wildly inconsistent**—as if the coastline itself changed.

* The **Black Reef Corsairs** claim descent from them, but likely just repurpose their ruins.

---

### **🧱 3\. The Most Recent Settlement (\~35 years ago)**

Modern interest began after a **visible Reckoning** shook the island and revealed Relics along the southern cliffs.

* The **Remnant Brotherhood** arrived first (see previous timeline), establishing **Kiremon’s Landing**.

* The **Ironbound Syndicate** followed soon after, building **Port Brayne**.

* The **Veiled Assembly** grew as a reaction—formed by settlers, mystics, and early defectors.

* The **Church of the Eternal Flame** arrived only in the past **4 years**.

* The **Nightweb**'s roots began when Syndicate and Brotherhood operations needed shadow networks.

Despite their years here, none of the factions have truly tamed the island—**they are only the latest in a long series of failures**.

---

### **🌒 Why No One Lasts**

* **Reckonings reset reality**: Paths change, places disappear, people vanish or return... wrong.

* **Relics resist domination**: They distort the land when misused—or even noticed too often.

* **The Island “Heals” itself**: Structures crumble, maps become lies, settlements are slowly erased by flora, fauna, and time-bent weather.

Even the **current factions** operate like **camps**, **outposts**, or **forward bases**—they don’t own the island. They survive on borrowed time.

## **🕰️ Recent History of Tharros (Past \~40 Years, Fine-Tuned)**

### **📍 \~40 years ago — The Shatterwake**
<!-- CATEGORY: LORE, SECRET -->

A great quake split part of the island’s southern cliffs, exposing **unnatural structures** humming with strange energy. Local sailors witnessed impossible geometry, flickering sky-lights, and time-lost wildlife. Word spread fast.

* Locals fled or went mad. A few stayed behind and formed the seeds of what would become the **Veiled Assembly**.

* Rumors of **Relics**—divine, technological, or both—drew outside attention.

*The island’s core remained untouched. The Shatterwake affected only a narrow coastal region.*

---

### **📍 \~35–30 years ago — Arrival of the Remnant Brotherhood**

Lured by ancient myths and preliminary finds, the **Brotherhood** sent expeditions to establish camps near the Shatterwake ruins.

* They set up **Kiremon’s Landing**, building sealed archives and survey towers.

* Within a few years, they began **hoarding Relics** and knowledge, pushing deeper inland with hired dig teams.

* Expeditions started going missing—either due to Reckonings or native interference.

*The Brotherhood’s progress stagnated the deeper they went—terrain, strange effects, and unknown forces blocked safe expansion.*

---

### **📍 \~28–22 years ago — Ironbound Syndicate Exploitation Phase**

The **Syndicate** arrived next, sensing profit in untapped ore, old alloys, and Relic salvage. They built **Port Brayne**, hired mercenaries, and offered jobs to desperate settlers.

* This influx ignited tension with the Brotherhood, who viewed the Syndicate’s operations as reckless.

* Deforestation and excavation grew rapidly. **Micro-Reckonings** began spiking in frequency.

* The **Veiled Assembly** formed in opposition—initially peaceful, but radicalized as lands were torn open.

*The Syndicate never fully mapped the interior. Beyond their logging zones, the land “twists.”*

---

### **📍 \~20–10 years ago — Entrenchment & Resistance**

The factions hardened their positions:

* **The Brotherhood** focused on consolidating data, restricting access to “volatile zones.”

* **The Syndicate** shifted toward security and self-reliance, arming their workers and reinforcing their camps.

* **The Assembly** disappeared into the highlands and wilds, launching sabotage raids and **burning bridges** (literally and politically).

* **The Nightweb** (then called the **Black Shard**) rose within Syndicate black markets and Brotherhood backchannels.

*Entire hexes inland remain unknown. Old maps don’t match the land anymore—Reckonings warp the terrain subtly over time.*

---

### **📍 \~6 years ago — The Deep Pulse**

A Brotherhood dig triggered a major Reckoning at **Spire Basin**. For a full day, the sky dimmed and shimmered. Stars “fell upward.” Several ruins collapsed or restructured.

* This event became known as the **Deep Pulse**.

* Many believe it “woke” parts of the island.

* Factions doubled down—investing or withdrawing depending on their doctrine.

*No expedition has successfully reached the core plateau since. All attempts either disappear or return... changed.*

---

### **📍 \~4 years ago — Arrival of the Church of the Eternal Flame**

The **Church** established a foothold at **Ashlight Monastery**, claiming the island as a place of trial, prophecy, and renewal.

* At first welcomed by settlers, their missionaries quickly clashed with the Brotherhood and Assembly.

* Some claim the Flame’s priests seek **Relics to sanctify**; others believe they aim to destroy them.

* A radical splinter group—the **Flamebound Zealots**—formed shortly after.

*The Church has little access to the deep wilds, often relying on diplomacy—or coercion—to expand.*

---

### **📍 Present Day — A Tense Equilibrium**

Factions circle each other, striking covertly or through proxies. Newcomers—explorers, criminals, refugees—arrive regularly. Many disappear.

* The **Nightweb**, now fully rebranded from the Black Shard, thrives on this instability.

* Most of the **interior remains unknown**, reshaped or hidden by natural camouflage and Relic interference.

* **The island is watching**—Reckonings are increasing, and something in the deep heart of Tharros stirs.
